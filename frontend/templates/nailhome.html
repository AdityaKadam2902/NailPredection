17 Detectable Conditions

Darier's Disease

Moderate

Autosomal dominant genodermatosis causing abnormal keratinization in nails and skin. Characteristic V-shaped nicks at the free edge.

Nail pittingRed & white streaksV-shaped notches

Muehrcke's Lines

Low

Paired white transverse bands associated with hypoalbuminemia. Bands do not indent the nail surface and disappear with pressure.

Parallel white linesTransverse bands

Alopecia Areata

Moderate

Autoimmune disorder affecting hair follicles with nail manifestations in roughly 10–38% of patients.

Nail pittingTrachyonychiaKoilonychia

Beau's Lines

Low

Transverse grooves caused by temporary disruption of nail matrix growth, typically from systemic illness, trauma, or chemotherapy.

Horizontal groovesMultiple nails affected

Bluish Nail

High

Cyanosis of the nail bed indicating peripheral hypoxia, cardiovascular compromise, or Raynaud's phenomenon. Warrants urgent evaluation.

Blue nail bedsCold extremitiesPoor capillary refill

Clubbing

High

Hypertrophic osteoarthropathy producing enlarged fingertip pads and increased nail curvature. Associated with pulmonary, cardiac, and hepatic disease.

Bulbous fingertipsSpongy nail baseIncreased nail angle

Eczema

Moderate

Atopic or contact dermatitis affecting the periungual tissue and nail fold, leading to secondary nail plate changes.

Inflamed nail foldNail thickeningOnycholysis

Lindsay's Nails

Moderate

Half-and-half nails: proximal white band and distal pink/brown band. A recognized clinical sign of chronic kidney disease.

Bicolor appearanceWhite proximal bandBrown distal band

Koilonychia

Low

Spoon-shaped nail deformity with central concavity. Classically associated with iron deficiency anemia; also seen in hemochromatosis and Plummer–Vinson syndrome.

Concave nail surfaceThin brittle nailsSpoon shape

Leukonychia

Low

White discoloration of the nail plate, most commonly from minor trauma to the nail matrix. Usually grows out without intervention.

White spotsWhite transverse streaks

Onycholysis

Moderate

Separation of the nail plate from the nail bed, beginning distally and progressing proximally. Caused by trauma, psoriasis, thyroid disease, or fungal infection.

Nail plate separationWhite distal edgeOdor if infected

Pale Nail

Moderate

Diffuse pallor of the nail bed, associated with anemia, malnutrition, peripheral vascular insufficiency, or liver disease.

Reduced nail bed colorOverall pallor

Red Lunula

Moderate

Erythematous discoloration of the normally white lunula. Reported in association with cardiac failure, alopecia areata, and collagen vascular diseases.

Red half-moon at baseAltered lunula color

Splinter Hemorrhage

Low

Dark red or brown linear streaks running longitudinally under the nail plate. Usually from minor trauma; multiple lesions raise suspicion for infective endocarditis.

Dark linear streaksLongitudinal orientation

Terry's Nail

High

Predominantly white nail plate with a narrow pink or reddish distal band (1–3 mm). A recognized hepatic marker, particularly in cirrhosis and hepatic failure.

White nail platePink distal bandLoss of lunula

White Nail

Moderate

Complete whitening of the nail plate. Can indicate liver disease, renal failure, thyroid disorders, or be a side effect of certain medications.

Total white appearanceOpaque plate

Yellow Nails

Moderate

Slow-growing, thickened, yellow-green nails often with onycholysis. Associated with lymphedema and chronic respiratory disease; a triad with pleural effusion defines Yellow Nail Syndrome.

Yellow-green colorThickened plateSlow growth
